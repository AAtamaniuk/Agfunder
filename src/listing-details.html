Summary

Green Collar Foods (GCF) is a controlled environment agriculture (CEA) company deploying an innovative network of franchised inner-city farming facilities. GCF aims to become the market leader for small-scale, locally-owned indoor food production systems across the United States and United Kingdom.

The GCF team has spent the last 3 years building its facility pipeline and in R&D for its aeroponic growing system with connected Microsoft Azure cloud-based control and a monitoring system. This system can be deployed in a 5,000 sqft operating space for $300k startup cost (including the building) in deprived inner-city areas. To complement this strategy, the company has developed integrated partnerships with major land-grant universities, both as long-term food purchase customers, and low cost providers of research and support.

In the next five years GCF's plan is to create 130 new inner city jobs and redevelop 200,000 sqft of blighted urban infrastructure, by supplying thousands of pounds of high-value specialty crops to local institutional customers and generating strong returns for investors.

Investment Highlights

  • GCF Platform generates multiple revenue streams: licensing IP, brokering forward-purchase contracts, monetizing CEA dataset across private industry and academia, and construction/project management fees
  • GCF Hubs feature low start-up costs by securing real estate in collaboration with local governments, utilizing in-house engineering expertise coupled with off-the-shelf designed equipment and labor efficiencies from partnerships
  • GCF has partnerships with major public land-grant universities on grow data, ag education, ag research and technology at Florida A&M, University of Sheffield and University of Connecticut
  • Beta hub (450 sqft) with three tier vertical farm operating in downtown Detroit for 12 months growing high quality kale and cilantro and delivering to local restaurants
  • Five GCF Hubs (5,000+ sqft operating space) in 1-year development pipeline: (1) Detroit, MI, (2) Bridgeport, CT, (3) Tallahassee, FL, (4) Sheffield, UK and (5) Pittsburgh, PA
  • Co-Founders have invested $700,000 of their own capital and services-in-kind over 3 years to successfully incubate GCF

CEO & Founder

Yogi has more than 15 years of technical development and management experience and is the inventor of a patent-pending, off-grid remote monitoring platform currently deployed in several mission critical infrastructure projects. Prior to Gotham, Mr. Sookhu led the technical development of two early stage companies, which resulted in a favorable acquisition and a fully matured, thriving company. Gerald Cross

Chief Civil Engineer P.E.

Gerald served as the Regional Engineer for the Federal Regulatory Energy Commission (FERC) for 23 years before retiring and joining Gotham. While at FERC Jerry was responsible for ensuring that dam owners and licensees complied with Federal mandates for the safe operation of Hydroelectric dams. Rob Maki

General Counsel

Rob has more than 15 years experience working in the technology industry in a variety of technical and legal roles. He has served as a software developer, an engineer for start-ups in Silicon Valley and Toronto, an attorney at some of Canada's most prestigious law firms, and has advised numerous tech start-ups in obtaining financing and in pursuing strategic initiatives.
